# Content Safety Redaction

Use this skill to create public-safe content drafts from private source material.

## Procedure

1. Identify the intended audience, publication channel, and claim being supported.
2. Convert private evidence into generalized lessons, paraphrases, or sanitized examples.
3. Remove raw transcripts, private names, local paths, credentials, account IDs, unreleased counterpart details, and private third-party quotes.
4. Preserve source attribution privately so the principal can audit where claims came from.
5. Route any publishable draft through exact-text approval.

## Guardrails

- Do not quote raw private chats or interviews in public drafts.
- Do not publish or schedule publication directly.
- Escalate when redaction would change the truth of the claim.
